This love is to be comprehensive, involving heart (emotions and will), soul (life itself), and strength (all our resources and abilities). The Shema points forward to how Christ enables us to love God completely through the Holy Spirit.

The Unity of God

This unity of God is fully revealed in the Trinityâ€”one God in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. The Shema declares God's unity, which is perfectly revealed in the Trinity. The Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are one God, yet distinct persons. Loving God with All Our Heart

The Shema calls us to love God with all our heartâ€”with our emotions, desires, and will. This is not a superficial affection but a deep, wholehearted devotion. The heart represents the center of our being, where our affections and decisions originate. To love God with all our heart means He has first place in our affections and desires.

You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, with all your mind, and with all your strength. Mark 12:30

Jesus expands the Shema to include "all your mind," showing that our love for God must engage our intellect as well. We cannot love God fully without understanding who He is and what He has done. Through Christ, we are enabled to love God with all our heart because He first loved us and gave Himself for us. The Holy Spirit works in our hearts to produce this love.

Teaching the Next Generation

The Shema includes instructions to teach these words to children, to talk about them constantly, and to make them visible reminders. This shows that love for God is not just personal but must be passed on to the next generation. The Shema is to be on our hearts, impressed on our children, talked about in daily life, and displayed as reminders.

Passing on the Faith

The Shema's instructions to teach children and make the law visible show that faith is meant to be shared. We are to pass on our love for God to the next generation, teaching them to love God with all their heart, soul, and strength. This points forward to how the church is called to make disciples, teaching them to observe all that Christ commanded.

Matthew 28:19-20
And these words which I command you today shall be in your heart. You shall teach them diligently to your children, and shall talk of them when you sit in your house, when you walk by the way, when you lie down, and when you rise up. Deuteronomy 6:6-7

The Shema's call to teach children and make the law visible shows that love for God is not private but public. We are to live out our love for God in such a way that others, especially our children, see it and learn from it. This points forward to how the church is called to make disciples, teaching them to love God and obey His commands. The Shema's emphasis on teaching the next generation finds its fulfillment in the Great Commission.

Conclusion: The Shema Fulfilled in Christ

The Shema is the greatest commandment, calling us to love God with all our heart, soul, and strength. It declares God's unity and calls for wholehearted devotion. Through Christ, we are enabled to fulfill the Shema because He first loved us and gave Himself for us. The Holy Spirit works in our hearts to produce this love.

The Shema's call to love God completely finds its fulfillment in Christ, who loved the Father perfectly and enables us to love God through the Holy Spirit. We don't love God to earn His favorâ€”we love Him because we have received His favor in Christ. The Shema, given to Israel in Deuteronomy, points forward to how Christ enables us to love God with all our heart, soul, mind, and strength.
