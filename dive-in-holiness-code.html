The Call to Holiness: Be Holy, For I Am Holy

The Holiness Code in Leviticus 19 begins with God's foundational command: "Be holy, for I am holy." This call to holiness was not merely about following external rulesâ€”it was about reflecting God's character in every aspect of life. The commands covered worship, family relationships, business ethics, treatment of the poor, and justice. This comprehensive call to holiness points forward to Christ, who perfectly fulfilled God's law and enables us to be holy through His work.

But as He who called you is holy, you also be holy in all your conduct, because it is written: "Be holy, for I am holy." 1 Peter 1:15-16

The Holiness Code revealed God's standard of holiness and Israel's need to reflect that holiness in their daily lives. But this standard was impossible to perfectly keep, revealing the need for a Savior who could both fulfill the law and provide the holiness we need. Christ is that Savior, who lived a perfectly holy life and provides His holiness to us.

Christ Our Holiness: The Perfect Fulfillment

We cannot achieve holiness on our own. The Holiness Code showed Israel what holiness looked like, but it also revealed their inability to perfectly keep it. Christ came as the perfect fulfillment of the law, living a life of complete holiness in thought, word, and deed. Through His work, we are not only declared holy (justified) but are also being made holy (sanctified).

Holiness Through Christ

Christ is our holiness. He perfectly kept every command of the Holiness Code, never failing in any area. Through His death and resurrection, He provides His holiness to us. We are declared holy in Him, and the Holy Spirit works in us to make us holy in practice.

Love Your Neighbor as Yourself

The Holiness Code culminates with the command to "love your neighbor as yourself" (Leviticus 19:18). This command, which Jesus called the second greatest commandment, shows that holiness is not just about avoiding sin but about actively loving others. Christ perfectly demonstrated this love, laying down His life for us, and through His Spirit, we are enabled to love others as He loved us.

A new commandment I give to you, that you love one another; as I have loved you, that you also love one another. By this all will know that you are My disciples, if you have love for one another. John 13:34-35

Christ elevated the command to love your neighbor, showing that true holiness is expressed through love. He loved us first, even when we were enemies, and calls us to love others in the same way. The Holiness Code's command to love points forward to Christ's perfect love, which enables us to love others.

Holiness in Daily Life

The Holiness Code applied holiness to every area of lifeâ€”from how to treat parents and the elderly, to how to conduct business honestly, to how to care for the poor and foreigner. This shows that holiness is not just for special occasions but for everyday life. Christ lived this kind of comprehensive holiness, and through Him, we are called to live holy lives in all we do.

Comprehensive Holiness

The Holiness Code covered every aspect of life, showing that holiness is not compartmentalized but comprehensive. Christ lived this way, being holy in every area of His life. Through Him, we are called to the same comprehensive holiness, not by our own strength, but through the power of the Holy Spirit.

Colossians 3:17
And whatever you do in word or deed, do all in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through Him. Colossians 3:17

Every aspect of our lives is to be lived in holiness, just as the Holiness Code required. But now, through Christ, we have the power to live this way. His holiness is worked in us by the Holy Spirit, enabling us to reflect God's character in every area of life.

Conclusion: Holiness Through Christ

The Holiness Code reveals God's standard of holiness and our need for a Savior. Christ perfectly fulfilled the law's requirements and provides the holiness we need through His work. We are called to be holy, not by our own efforts, but through Christ who is our holiness.

As we abide in Him, His holiness is worked in us by the Holy Spirit, enabling us to live lives that reflect God's character. The Holiness Code that showed what holiness looks like points forward to Christ, who is our holiness and who enables us to live holy lives.
