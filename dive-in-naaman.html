God's Grace to the Nations

Naaman's Leprosy

In 2 Kings 5:1-27, Naaman, a commander of the Syrian army, has leprosy. A young Israelite girl tells Naaman's wife that there is a prophet in Israel who can heal him.

"Now Naaman was commander of the army of the king of Aram. He was a great man in the sight of his master and highly regarded, because through him the Lord had given victory to Aram. He was a valiant soldier, but he had leprosy. Now bands of raiders from Aram had gone out and had taken captive a young girl from Israel, and she served Naaman's wife. She said to her mistress, 'If only my master would see the prophet who is in Samaria! He would cure him of his leprosy.'" 2 Kings 5:1-3

Naaman is a powerful and respected commander, but he has leprosy, a disease that would eventually lead to his death and social isolation. A young Israelite girl, who was taken captive in a raid, serves Naaman's wife. Despite being a captive, she shows compassion and tells her mistress about the prophet Elisha who can heal Naaman. This demonstrates God's grace working through unexpected people.

Elisha's Simple Command

Naaman goes to Israel with a letter from the king of Syria. Elisha sends a message telling Naaman to wash in the Jordan River seven times. Naaman is initially angry because he expected a dramatic healing.

"So Naaman went with his horses and chariots and stopped at the door of Elisha's house. Elisha sent a messenger to say to him, 'Go, wash yourself seven times in the Jordan, and your flesh will be restored and you will be cleansed.' But Naaman went away angry and said, 'I thought that he would surely come out to me and stand and call on the name of the Lord his God, wave his hand over the spot and cure me of my leprosy.'" 2 Kings 5:9-11

Naaman arrives at Elisha's house with his horses and chariots, expecting a grand reception. Instead, Elisha doesn't even come out to meet him. He sends a messenger with simple instructions: wash in the Jordan River seven times. Naaman is furious. He expected Elisha to perform a dramatic ritual, calling on God's name and waving his hand. He also thinks the rivers of Damascus are better than the Jordan. However, Naaman's servants convince him to follow Elisha's instructions.

God's Grace to All Nations

Elisha's healing of Naaman points forward to how Christ brings salvation to all nations. Just as Naaman, a Gentile, was healed, Christ brings healing and salvation to people from all nations. This demonstrates that God's grace extends to all who believe, regardless of their background.

Naaman's Healing and Faith

When Naaman washes in the Jordan seven times, his leprosy is healed. He returns to Elisha and declares that there is no God in all the earth except the Lord.

"So he went down and dipped himself in the Jordan seven times, as the man of God had told him, and his flesh was restored and became clean like that of a young boy. Then he and all his attendants went back to the man of God. He stood before him and said, 'Now I know that there is no God in all the world except in Israel.'" 2 Kings 5:14-15

Naaman follows Elisha's instructions and washes in the Jordan River seven times. When he comes up from the water, his flesh is restored and becomes clean like that of a young boy. His leprosy is completely healed. Naaman returns to Elisha and declares his faith: "Now I know that there is no God in all the world except in Israel." He offers Elisha gifts, but Elisha refuses. Naaman asks for two mule-loads of earth so he can worship the Lord on Israelite soil, and he promises to worship only the Lord from now on.

This event demonstrates God's grace to Gentiles and points forward to how Christ brings salvation to all nations. Just as Naaman, a Gentile, was healed, Christ brings healing and salvation to people from all nations. This demonstrates that God's grace extends to all who believe, regardless of their background.
