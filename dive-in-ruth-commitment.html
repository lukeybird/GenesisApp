Faith and Loyalty Beyond Measure

Ruth's Famous Declaration

In Ruth 1:1-22, we witness one of the most beautiful expressions of loyalty and faith in all of Scripture. After losing her husband, brother-in-law, and father-in-law, Ruth faces a choice: return to her Moabite family and their gods, or follow Naomi to Bethlehem and commit to the God of Israel. Ruth chooses faith, making a declaration that has echoed through the ages.

But Ruth said: "Entreat me not to leave you, or to turn back from following after you; for wherever you go, I will go; and wherever you lodge, I will lodge; your people shall be my people, and your God, my God. Where you die, I will die, and there I will be buried. The Lord do so to me, and more also, if anything but death parts you and me." Ruth 1:16-17

Ruth's commitment is extraordinary. As a Moabite, she was outside the covenant people of Israel. Yet she chooses to leave her homeland, her people, and her gods to follow Naomi and commit to the God of Israel.
