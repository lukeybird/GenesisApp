Seeing Christ as the Living Water

Christ: The Rock That Was Struck

When Moses struck the rock at Horeb and water flowed out for Israel to drink (Exodus 17:1-7), this was a powerful picture of Christ, who was "struck" on the cross so that living water might flow to all who believe. The apostle Paul explicitly identifies the rock as Christ.

And all drank the same spiritual drink. For they drank of that spiritual Rock that followed them, and that Rock was Christ. 1 Corinthians 10:4

Paul explicitly tells us that the rock that provided water for Israel was Christ. Just as the rock was struck and water flowed out, so Christ was struck on the cross and the living water of the Holy Spirit flows out to all who believe. The rock that was struck points to Christ who was crucified, and the water that flowed points to the Holy Spirit who gives life.

The Struck Rock: Christ Crucified

Moses was commanded to strike the rock, and water would flow out. This striking of the rock points to Christ being struck on the cross. Just as the rock had to be struck for water to flow, so Christ had to be crucified for the Holy Spirit to be given.

But He was wounded for our transgressions, He was bruised for our iniquities; the chastisement for our peace was upon Him, and by His stripes we are healed. Isaiah 53:5

Christ was struck for us on the cross, just as the rock was struck for Israel. The rock had to be struck once; Christ was struck once for all. The water flowed from the struck rock; the Holy Spirit flows from the crucified Christ. The striking was necessary for the water to flow; the cross was necessary for the Spirit to be given.

The water from the rock satisfied Israel's physical thirst temporarily, but they would thirst again. Christ offers water that satisfies our spiritual thirst forever. Just as the water from the rock was a temporary provision, so all earthly provisions are temporary. But Christ gives us the Holy Spirit, who satisfies our deepest spiritual needs eternally.

The Rock That Followed Them

Paul tells us that the rock "followed them" through the wilderness (1 Corinthians 10:4). This speaks to the continual presence of Christ with His people. Just as the rock was always available to provide water, so Christ is always available to provide the Holy Spirit to all who believe.

And all drank the same spiritual drink. For they drank of that spiritual Rock that followed them, and that Rock was Christ. 1 Corinthians 10:4

The rock that followed Israel points to Christ, who is always with His people. Just as the rock was always available to provide water, so Christ is always available to provide the Holy Spirit. The rock followed them through the wilderness; Christ is with us through all of life. The rock was struck once; Christ was crucified once, and the Holy Spirit flows from Him forever.

Conclusion: The Rock That Gives Living Water

The water from the rock was a miraculous provision that sustained Israel in the wilderness, but it was temporary and could not give eternal life. Christ is the true Rock who gives the Holy Spirit, the living water that gives eternal life. The rock that was struck points to Christ who was crucified, and the water that flowed points to the Holy Spirit who gives life.

Just as the rock had to be struck for water to flow, so Christ had to be crucified for the Holy Spirit to be given. Just as the water from the rock satisfied physical thirst temporarily, so the Holy Spirit satisfies spiritual thirst forever. And just as the rock followed Israel through the wilderness, so Christ is always with us, providing the living water of the Holy Spirit to all who believe.
