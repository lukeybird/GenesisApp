Victory Through Faith and Obedience

Rahab's Salvation by Faith

Rahab, a Canaanite prostitute, demonstrates that salvation is by faith, not by works or heritage. She believed in the God of Israel and acted on that faith by hiding the spies. When Jericho fell, she and her family were saved because of her faith. Rahab's story shows that God's grace extends to all who believe, regardless of their background or past.

By faith the harlot Rahab did not perish with those who did not believe, when she had received the spies with peace. Hebrews 11:31

Rahab's faith saved her and her family. She believed in the God of Israel even though she was a Gentile, and her faith was counted as righteousness. Rahab becomes part of God's people and is even listed in the genealogy of Jesus, showing that God's grace extends to all who believe, regardless of their past or background.
