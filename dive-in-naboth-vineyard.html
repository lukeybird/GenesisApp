Coveting Leads to Murder

Ahab's Coveting

In 1 Kings 21:1-29, King Ahab covets Naboth's vineyard, which is next to his palace. When Naboth refuses to sell it, Ahab becomes sullen and refuses to eat.

"Naboth the Jezreelite had a vineyard in Jezreel, beside the palace of Ahab king of Samaria. Ahab said to Naboth, 'Let me have your vineyard to use for a vegetable garden, since it is close to my palace. In exchange I will give you a better vineyard or, if you prefer, I will pay you whatever it is worth.' But Naboth replied, 'The Lord forbid that I should give you the inheritance of my ancestors.'" 1 Kings 21:1-3

Ahab wants Naboth's vineyard because it is next to his palace. He offers to buy it or trade for a better vineyard, but Naboth refuses because it is his family inheritance. According to Israelite law, land could not be permanently sold because it belonged to God and was assigned to families. Naboth is right to refuse, but Ahab becomes sullen and refuses to eat, acting like a spoiled child.

Jezebel's Scheme

When Ahab tells Jezebel about Naboth's refusal, she schemes to have Naboth falsely accused and stoned to death so Ahab can take the vineyard.

"So she wrote letters in Ahab's name, placed his seal on them, and sent them to the elders and nobles who lived in Naboth's city with him. In those letters she wrote: 'Proclaim a day of fasting and seat Naboth in a prominent place among the people. But seat two scoundrels opposite him and have them bring charges that he has cursed both God and the king. Then take him out and stone him to death.'" 1 Kings 21:8-10

Jezebel takes matters into her own hands. She writes letters in Ahab's name, instructing the elders to falsely accuse Naboth of cursing God and the king, which was a capital offense. The elders carry out her plan, and Naboth is stoned to death. After Naboth is killed, Ahab goes to take possession of the vineyard. This demonstrates how coveting leads to greater sins, including murder.

Christ's Innocence

Ahab's murder of Naboth points forward to how Christ was killed by those who coveted power and position. However, Christ's death provides forgiveness for all sin, including the sin of coveting. Through Christ, we are freed from the power of sin.

"Then the word of the Lord came to Elijah the Tishbite: 'Go down to meet Ahab king of Israel, who rules in Samaria. He is now in Naboth's vineyard, where he has gone to take possession of it. Say to him, "This is what the Lord says: Have you not murdered a man and seized his property?" Elijah confronts Ahab with his sin and pronounces God's judgment. Ahab's family will be destroyed, and dogs will lick up Ahab's blood in the same place where they licked up Naboth's blood. This judgment demonstrates the seriousness of coveting and murder. However, when Ahab humbles himself, God shows mercy and delays the judgment.
