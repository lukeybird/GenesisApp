God's Holiness: A Warning to All

The death of Nadab and Abihu in Leviticus 10:1-7 is one of the most sobering events in Scripture. These sons of Aaron, newly ordained as priests, offered "unauthorized fire" before the Lord and were immediately consumed by fire from God's presence. This event demonstrates the absolute holiness of God and the seriousness of approaching Him on our own terms.

Among those who approach me I will be proved holy; in the sight of all the people I will be honored. Leviticus 10:3

Moses declared to Aaron after the death of his sons: "This is what the Lord spoke of when He said: 'Among those who approach me I will be proved holy; in the sight of all the people I will be honored.'" This event reveals that God's holiness is not negotiable. We cannot approach God on our own terms, with our own methods, or in our own way. God must be approached according to His commands, through the means He has provided.

Nadab and Abihu's death was immediate and severe. They offered unauthorized fireâ€”fire that was not taken from the altar as God commanded. Conclusion: Approaching God Through Christ

The death of Nadab and Abihu teaches us that we cannot approach God on our own terms. Their unauthorized fire represents all our attempts to come to God through our own works, our own methods, or our own righteousness. These attempts always fail because they do not honor God's holiness.

But Christ has provided the way. He is our perfect High Priest who has done everything according to God's commands. Through His blood, we can approach God's throne with confidence, not with fear. The death of Nadab and Abihu warns us of the cost of irreverence, but Christ's death provides us with the way of salvation.
