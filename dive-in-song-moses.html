A Song of Witness

The Law Given Again to a New Generation

In Deuteronomy 5, Moses restates the Ten Commandments to the new generation of Israelites who are about to enter the Promised Land. This generation was not present at Mount Sinai when the law was first given; they were either children or not yet born. Moses emphasizes that the covenant was made with them, not just their ancestors, showing that God's law is for every generation.

The Lord our God made a covenant with us in Horeb. The Lord did not make this covenant with our fathers, but with us, those who are here today, all of us who are alive. Deuteronomy 5:2-3

Moses' repetition of the Ten Commandments shows that God's law is not outdated or irrelevantâ€”it is for every generation. Just as Moses restated the law for the new generation, the law points forward to how Christ fulfills and establishes the law for all who believe in Him. The law reveals God's holy character and our need for a Savior.

The Law Reveals God's Holy Character

The Ten Commandments reveal the character of the God who gave them. They show us what God is likeâ€”holy, just, loving, and faithful. The first four commandments show us how to relate to God, while the last six show us how to relate to others. Together, they reveal God's perfect standard of righteousness.

God's Perfect Standard

The Ten Commandments reveal God's perfect standard, which no human can keep perfectly. This shows us our need for a Savior who can keep the law perfectly on our behalf. Christ fulfilled every requirement of the law, and through faith in Him, His perfect obedience is credited to us.

Romans 3:23
For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, being justified freely by His grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus. Romans 3:23-24

The Ten Commandments reveal that we all fall short of God's perfect standard. We cannot keep the law perfectly, which shows us our need for Christ. He kept the law perfectly and died to pay the penalty for our law-breaking. Through faith in Him, we are justified and enabled by the Holy Spirit to live according to God's law.

Christ Fulfills the Law

Jesus said He came not to destroy the law but to fulfill it (Matthew 5:17). Christ kept every commandment perfectly, something no human has ever done. His perfect obedience to the law is credited to all who believe in Him, and through the Holy Spirit, we are enabled to live according to God's law.

Do not think that I came to destroy the Law or the Prophets. I did not come to destroy but to fulfill. For assuredly, I say to you, till heaven and earth pass away, one jot or one tittle will by no means pass from the law till all is fulfilled. Matthew 5:17-18

Christ fulfilled the law in two ways: He kept it perfectly in His life, and He paid the penalty for our law-breaking in His death. Through faith in Him, His perfect obedience is credited to us, and we are declared righteous. The Holy Spirit then enables us to live according to God's law, not to earn salvation, but as a response to the grace we have received.

The Law Written on Our Hearts

In the New Covenant, God promises to write His law on our hearts (Jeremiah 31:33). This is fulfilled in Christ through the Holy Spirit, who enables us to love God and others from the heart. The law is no longer external rules we must follow, but internal principles that guide our lives.

The Spirit Enables Obedience

The Holy Spirit enables us to keep God's law from the heart. We don't obey to earn God's favorâ€”we obey because we have received God's favor in Christ. The Spirit gives us the desire and ability to live according to God's law, showing that we are truly His people.

Philippians 2:13
For it is God who works in you both to will and to do for His good pleasure. Philippians 2:13

Through the Holy Spirit, God works in us to give us both the desire and the ability to keep His law. This is not about external compliance but internal transformation. The law written on our hearts means we love God and others from the heart, not just follow rules. This is the fulfillment of what Moses restated in Deuteronomyâ€”the law for life in the land becomes the law written on our hearts in Christ.

Conclusion: The Law Fulfilled in Christ

Moses' restatement of the Ten Commandments in Deuteronomy shows that God's law is for every generation. The law reveals God's holy character, our inability to keep it perfectly, and our need for a Savior. Christ fulfills the law perfectly, and through faith in Him, His perfect obedience is credited to us.

The law is now written on our hearts by the Holy Spirit, enabling us to love God and others from the heart. We don't obey to earn salvationâ€”we obey because we have received salvation in Christ. The Ten Commandments, restated by Moses for the new generation, point forward to how Christ fulfills and establishes the law for all who believe in Him.
