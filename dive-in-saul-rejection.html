Disobedience Leads to Rejection

Partial Obedience Is Disobedience

In 1 Samuel 15:1-35, we see the tragic consequences of partial obedience. God commands Saul to completely destroy the Amalekites, but Saul disobeys, sparing King Agag and the best of the livestock. This act of disobedience leads to Saul's rejection as king, demonstrating that partial obedience is disobedience.

But Samuel replied: "Does the Lord delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices as much as in obeying the Lord? To obey is better than sacrifice, and to heed is better than the fat of rams. For rebellion is like the sin of divination, and arrogance like the evil of idolatry. Because you have rejected the word of the Lord, he has rejected you as king." 1 Samuel 15:22-23

Saul's disobedience demonstrates a critical truth: God requires complete obedience. Saul thought he could partially obey and make up for it with sacrifices, but God rejected this.
