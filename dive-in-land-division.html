Each Tribe Receives Its Inheritance

In Joshua 13-21, after the conquest, Joshua divides the Promised Land among the twelve tribes of Israel. Each tribe receives its portion by lot, showing that the division is according to God's will, not human preference. This fulfills God's promise to Abraham that his descendants would inherit the land of Canaan.

These are the areas which the children of Israel inherited in the land of Canaan, which Eleazar the priest, Joshua the son of Nun, and the heads of the fathers of the tribes of the children of Israel distributed as an inheritance to them. Their inheritance was by lot, as the Lord had commanded by the hand of Moses, for the nine tribes and the half-tribe. Joshua 14:1-2

The division of the land by lot shows that God determines each tribe's inheritance. This demonstrates that God is faithful to His promisesâ€”He promised the land to Abraham, and He gave it to his descendants. Each tribe received its portion, ensuring that all of Israel had a place in the Promised Land.

God's Faithfulness to Promises

The division of the land fulfills God's promise to Abraham that his descendants would inherit the land of Canaan. God made this promise centuries earlier, and now He fulfills it completely. Each tribe receives its inheritance, showing that God is faithful to keep His promises, no matter how long it takes.

The Levites did not receive a land inheritance like the other tribes. Instead, the Lord Himself was their inheritance. They received cities to live in, but no land for farming or herding. This shows that their service to God was their inheritance, pointing forward to how our true inheritance is not earthly but heavenlyâ€”it is Christ Himself.

Our inheritance is not in this world but in the world to come, and it is Christ Himself.
