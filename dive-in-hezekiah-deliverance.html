Deliverance Through Faith

The Assyrian Threat

In 2 Kings 18:13-19:37, the Assyrian king Sennacherib threatens Jerusalem. Hezekiah trusts in the Lord and prays for deliverance.

"The king of Assyria sent his supreme commander, his chief officer and his field commander with a large army from Lachish to King Hezekiah at Jerusalem. They came up to Jerusalem and stopped at the aqueduct of the Upper Pool, on the road to the Launderer's Field. They called for the king; and Eliakim son of Hilkiah the palace administrator, Shebna the secretary, and Joah son of Asaph the recorder went out to them." 2 Kings 18:17-18

The Assyrian king Sennacherib sends his commanders with a large army to Jerusalem. They stand outside the city and call for King Hezekiah. The Assyrian commander speaks in Hebrew so all the people can hear, trying to discourage them and turn them against Hezekiah. He mocks Hezekiah's trust in the Lord and claims that the Lord cannot save them.

Hezekiah's Prayer

When Hezekiah receives the Assyrian letter, he goes to the temple and spreads it out before the Lord, praying for deliverance.

"Hezekiah received the letter from the messengers and read it. Then he went up to the temple of the Lord and spread it out before the Lord. And Hezekiah prayed to the Lord: 'Lord, the God of Israel, enthroned between the cherubim, you alone are God over all the kingdoms of the earth. You have made heaven and earth. Give ear, Lord, and hear; open your eyes, Lord, and see; listen to the words Sennacherib has sent to ridicule the living God.'" 2 Kings 19:14-16

Hezekiah doesn't panic or try to negotiate. Instead, he goes to the temple and spreads the Assyrian letter before the Lord. He prays, acknowledging that the Lord alone is God over all the kingdoms of the earth. He asks the Lord to hear and see, and to listen to the words Sennacherib has sent to ridicule the living God. Hezekiah's prayer demonstrates his complete trust in the Lord.

Christ Our Deliverer

Hezekiah's deliverance points forward to how Christ delivers us from our enemies. Just as God delivered Jerusalem from the Assyrians, Christ delivers us from sin and death. Through faith in Christ, we are saved from the judgment we deserve.

The prophet Isaiah tells Hezekiah that God will deliver Jerusalem. That night, an angel of the Lord goes out and kills 185,000 Assyrian soldiers. When the people wake up the next morning, they see all the dead bodies. Sennacherib breaks camp and withdraws, returning to Nineveh. Later, while he is worshiping in the temple of his god, his own sons kill him with the sword. This demonstrates God's power to deliver His people and to judge those who mock Him.
