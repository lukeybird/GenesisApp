Providence in Action

Esther Chosen for Such a Time

In Esther 2:1-18, after Queen Vashti is removed, King Xerxes searches for a new queen. Esther, a beautiful Jewish orphan raised by her cousin Mordecai, is taken to the palace. She finds favor with everyone who sees her, including the king, who chooses her as queen. However, she keeps her Jewish identity secret, as Mordecai had instructed her. Esther's rise to queen demonstrates God's providential care, placing her in a position where she can later save her people. This points forward to how Christ was placed in a position of authority to save His people.

Now when the turn came for Esther the daughter of Abihail the uncle of Mordecai, who had taken her as his daughter, to go in to the king, she requested nothing but what Hegai the king's eunuch, the custodian of the women, advised. And Esther obtained favor in the sight of all who saw her. So Esther was taken to King Ahasuerus, into his royal palace, in the tenth month, which is the month of Tebeth, in the seventh year of his reign. The king loved Esther more than all the other women, and she obtained grace and favor in his sight more than all the virgins; so he set the royal crown upon her head and made her queen instead of Vashti. Esther 2:15-17
God's Providential Care

Esther's rise to queen was not by chance but by God's providential care. God placed her in a position of influence to save her people. This demonstrates that God works through ordinary circumstances to accomplish His purposes. Esther's story shows that God is always working behind the scenes, even when His name is not mentioned.

Esther Points to Christ

Esther's position as queen points forward to how Christ was placed in a position of authority to save His people. Just as Esther was chosen for such a time as this, Christ was sent at the right time to save us. Esther's story demonstrates that God's plan of salvation includes placing the right person in the right position at the right time.
