Preparing for God's House

David's Desire to Build the Temple

In 1 Chronicles 22:1-5, David expresses his desire to build a house for the Lord. However, God tells David that he cannot build the temple because he is a man of war and has shed blood. Instead, God promises that David's son Solomon will build the temple. David accepts this and begins making extensive preparations.

David said, "My son Solomon is young and inexperienced, and the house to be built for the Lord should be of great magnificence and fame and splendor in the sight of all the nations. Therefore I will make preparations for it." So David made extensive preparations before his death. 1 Chronicles 22:5

David's heart is set on building a house for the Lord, even though he cannot do it himself. His willingness to prepare for what he cannot complete points forward to how God prepared the way for Christ throughout history, and how we are called to prepare the way for Christ's return.

Gathering Materials and Organizing Workers

In 1 Chronicles 22:14-16, David gathers vast amounts of materials for the temple: gold, silver, bronze, iron, wood, and precious stones. He also organizes the priests and Levites, giving them specific assignments for the temple service. David's preparations are thorough and extensive.

Preparing for God's Work

David's careful preparation shows the importance of being ready for God's work. Just as David prepared materials and organized workers for the temple, God prepared everything for the coming of Christ, and we are called to prepare ourselves and others for Christ's work in the world.

David's Instructions to Solomon

In 1 Chronicles 28:1-21, David gives detailed instructions to Solomon about building the temple. He provides the plans that God gave him and charges Solomon to be strong and courageous and to build the temple according to God's specifications.

"Be strong and courageous, and do the work. Do not be afraid or discouraged, for the Lord God, my God, is with you. He will not fail you or forsake you until all the work for the service of the temple of the Lord is finished." 1 Chronicles 28:20

David's instructions to Solomon emphasize the importance of following God's plans exactly and trusting in God's presence and help. This points forward to how Christ builds the church according to God's plan and how we are called to build up the church, trusting in God's presence and power.

Conclusion: Preparing for the True Temple

David's extensive preparations for the temple demonstrate his wholehearted commitment to God's work. Although he cannot build the temple himself, he does everything he can to prepare for it. His preparations point forward to how God prepared the way for Christ throughout history.

Just as David prepared for the physical temple, God prepared for the coming of Christ, the true temple. Christ now builds the church, the true house of God, and we are called to participate in this work. David's preparations remind us that we are to prepare ourselves and others for Christ's work, building up the church as the temple of the Holy Spirit.
