Seeing Christ in the Beginning

The Foundation: In the Beginning Was the Word

The Creation account in Genesis 1-2 is not merely a historical record of how the universe came into beingâ€”it is the opening act of a grand narrative that finds its ultimate meaning and fulfillment in Jesus Christ. The New Testament explicitly reveals that the One who spoke creation into existence is the same One who would come to redeem it.

In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. He was in the beginning with God. All things were made through Him, and without Him nothing was made that was made. John 1:1-3

When Genesis opens with "In the beginning, God created," it points forward to the One who would be revealed as the Creator Himselfâ€”Jesus Christ. The apostle John deliberately echoes Genesis 1:1 to show that the Word who became flesh (John 1:14) is the same Word through whom all things were created.

Additional References
Revelation 19:13
Jesus is called "The Word of God" in His return, confirming His identity as the eternal Word.
1 John 1:1-2
John identifies Jesus as "the Word of life" who was "from the beginning," reinforcing His eternal nature.
Proverbs 8:22-31
Wisdom (personified) was present at creation, pointing to Christ as the wisdom of God who was with God in the beginning.

Jesus: The Agent of Creation

The New Testament makes it abundantly clear that Jesus Christ was not only present at creation but was the active agent through whom God created everything. This truth is stated explicitly in multiple passages:

The Creator in Colossians

Paul writes that Jesus is "the image of the invisible God, the firstborn over all creation. For by Him all things were created that are in heaven and that are on earth, visible and invisible" (Colossians 1:15-16). The phrase "firstborn" here doesn't mean Jesus was createdâ€”it means He has the rights and privileges of the firstborn, the preeminent position over all creation.

Colossians 1:15-17
The Sustainer of All Things

Not only did Jesus create all things, but He also sustains them. The writer of Hebrews declares that God "has in these last days spoken to us by His Son, whom He has appointed heir of all things, through whom also He made the worlds" (Hebrews 1:2). The same One who spoke the universe into existence continues to hold it together by His powerful word.

Hebrews 1:2-3
Additional References
1 Corinthians 8:6
Paul declares "one Lord Jesus Christ, through whom are all things," explicitly stating Christ's role in creation.
Ephesians 3:9
God "created all things through Jesus Christ," confirming Christ as the agent of creation.
Revelation 4:11
All things were created by God's will, and Christ is the expression of that will through whom creation came to be.

The Image of God: From Adam to Christ

Genesis 1:27 declares that God created humanity "in His own image; in the image of God He created him; male and female He created them." This profound truth finds its ultimate expression in Jesus Christ, who is described as "the image of the invisible God" (Colossians 1:15) and "the express image of His person" (Hebrews 1:3).

For whom He foreknew, He also predestined to be conformed to the image of His Son, that He might be the firstborn among many brethren. Romans 8:29

Where Adam failed to reflect God's image perfectly, Jesus perfectly reveals what it means to be the image of God. Through Christ, believers are being restored to the image that was marred by sin, ultimately to be conformed to the image of the Son.

Additional References
2 Corinthians 4:4
Christ is "the image of God," the perfect revelation of what God is like.
1 Corinthians 15:49
Believers will "bear the image of the heavenly Man" (Christ), being restored to the image lost in Adam.
Philippians 2:6
Christ existed "in the form of God," being the perfect expression of God's nature and image.

The First Adam and the Last Adam

Paul explicitly draws the connection between Adam and Christ, calling Jesus "the last Adam" (1 Corinthians 15:45). This typology reveals how the story of creation and fall points forward to redemption:

For as in Adam all die, even so in Christ all shall be made alive. 1 Corinthians 15:22
The Contrast

Romans 5:12-21 provides a detailed comparison: "Therefore, just as through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin, and thus death spread to all men, because all sinned... But the free gift is not like the offense. For if by the one man's offense many died, much more the grace of God and the gift by the grace of the one Man, Jesus Christ, abounded to many" (Romans 5:12, 15).

Romans 5:12-21

Adam brought death and separation from God; Christ brings life and reconciliation. Adam's disobedience brought condemnation; Christ's obedience brings justification. The first creation through Adam was corrupted; the new creation through Christ is being restored.

Additional References
1 Corinthians 15:21-22
"For since by man came death, by Man also came the resurrection of the dead. For as in Adam all die, even so in Christ all shall be made alive."
Philippians 2:8
Christ's obedience "to the point of death" contrasts with Adam's disobedience, showing perfect submission to God's will.
Romans 5:18
"Therefore, as through one man's offense judgment came to all men, resulting in condemnation, even so through one Man's righteous act the free gift came to all men, resulting in justification of life."

Light of the World

On the first day of creation, God said, "Let there be light" (Genesis 1:3), and light was separated from darkness. This physical light points to a greater spiritual realityâ€”Jesus Christ, who declared Himself to be "the light of the world" (John 8:12).

In Him was life, and the life was the light of men. And the light shines in the darkness, and the darkness did not comprehend it. John 1:4-5

Just as God's first creative act was to bring light into darkness, so Christ came as the true Light to dispel the spiritual darkness that had covered the earth. Those who follow Him "shall not walk in darkness, but have the light of life" (John 8:12).

Additional References
1 John 1:5
"God is light and in Him is no darkness at all," showing that Christ as the light reveals the nature of God Himself.
2 Corinthians 4:6
God "commanded light to shine out of darkness" in creation, and "has shone in our hearts to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ."
Ephesians 5:8
"You were once darkness, but now you are light in the Lord," showing that believers become partakers of Christ's light nature.

Sabbath Rest: Finding Rest in Christ

On the seventh day, God rested from all His work, blessing and sanctifying that day (Genesis 2:2-3). This Sabbath rest was not because God was tired, but because His work of creation was complete. This pattern of rest points forward to the ultimate rest found in Christ.

Come to Me, all you who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest. Take My yoke upon you and learn from Me, for I am gentle and lowly in heart, and you will find rest for your souls. Matthew 11:28-30
The True Sabbath Rest

The book of Hebrews explains that the Sabbath rest of creation points to a greater rest: "There remains therefore a rest for the people of God. For he who has entered His rest has himself also ceased from his works as God did from His" (Hebrews 4:9-10). This rest is found in Christ, who completed the work of redemption, just as God completed the work of creation.

Hebrews 4:1-11
Additional References
Mark 2:27-28
Jesus declares Himself "Lord even of the Sabbath," showing that the Sabbath rest finds its fulfillment in Him.
Hebrews 4:3
"We who have believed do enter that rest," showing that faith in Christ brings us into the true Sabbath rest.
Matthew 11:28
Christ's invitation to "come to Me... and I will give you rest" fulfills the promise of Sabbath rest found in creation.

New Creation: The Ultimate Fulfillment

The story that begins with "In the beginning, God created" finds its ultimate fulfillment in the promise of a new creation. Just as God created the heavens and the earth in the beginning, so He promises to create "new heavens and a new earth" (Isaiah 65:17, Revelation 21:1).

Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new. 2 Corinthians 5:17
Now I saw a new heaven and a new earth, for the first heaven and the first earth had passed away. Also there was no more sea. Then I, John, saw the holy city, New Jerusalem, coming down out of heaven from God, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband. Revelation 21:1-5

Through Christ, believers become part of this new creation. The old order, corrupted by sin, is passing away. The new order, redeemed and restored through the work of Christ, is already breaking in and will be fully realized when He returns. The creation that began in Genesis finds its completion in the new creation that Christ brings.

Additional References
Galatians 6:15
"For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision nor uncircumcision avails anything, but a new creation," showing that new creation is the essence of salvation.
Ephesians 4:24
Believers are to "put on the new man which was created according to God, in true righteousness and holiness," reflecting the new creation nature.
Isaiah 43:19
"Behold, I will do a new thing," pointing forward to the new creation that God would bring through Christ.

Conclusion: Christ in the Beginning

The Creation account is not a standalone story but the opening chapter of a narrative that finds its center and meaning in Jesus Christ. He is the Word through whom all things were created, the true Image of God, the Last Adam who reverses the curse of the first, the Light that dispels darkness, the Rest for the weary, and the Author of the new creation.

When we read Genesis 1-2, we are not just reading about how the world beganâ€”we are reading about the One who was there in the beginning, who came to redeem what was lost, and who will one day make all things new. The story of creation is, from the very first verse, the story of Christ.

Additional References
Revelation 21:5
"Behold, I make all things new," spoken by the One who was there in the beginning, completing the story that started in Genesis.
Colossians 1:18
Christ is "the firstborn from the dead," the beginning of the new creation, just as He was the beginning of the first creation.
John 1:18
"No one has seen God at any time. The only begotten Son... has declared Him," showing that Christ reveals the God who created all things.
