Conclusion: The Ultimate Sacrifice

The binding of Isaac is a profound picture of the gospel. Abraham's willingness to offer his son points to God's actual offering of His Son. Isaac's carrying of the wood points to Christ carrying His cross. The ram provided by God points to Christ, the Lamb God provided. And Mount Moriah, the place of sacrifice, points to Golgotha, where the ultimate sacrifice was made.

But there is one crucial difference: Abraham's hand was stayed; God's was not. Isaac was spared; Christ was not. The ram was a substitute for Isaac; Christ became the substitute for us. In this story, we see the gospel in shadow formâ€”and in Christ, we see it in full light.
