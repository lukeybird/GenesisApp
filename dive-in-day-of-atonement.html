Christ Enters Heaven: Our Great High Priest

The Day of Atonement in Leviticus 16 was the most sacred day in Israel's calendar. Only on this day could the high priest enter the Most Holy Place to make atonement for the nation's sins. This annual event pointed forward to Christ, who entered heaven itself as our great High Priest, making atonement once for all.

But Christ came as High Priest of the good things to come, with the greater and more perfect tabernacle not made with hands, that is, not of this creation. Not with the blood of goats and calves, but with His own blood He entered the Most Holy Place once for all, having obtained eternal redemption. Hebrews 9:11-12

Just as the high priest entered the Most Holy Place once a year on the Day of Atonement, Christ entered heaven itself once for all. Unlike the earthly high priest who had to repeat this act annually, Christ's entrance was final and complete. He obtained eternal redemption, not temporary atonement that needed to be repeated.

The Two Goats: Sacrifice and Scapegoat

On the Day of Atonement, two goats were used. One was sacrificed as a sin offering, its blood sprinkled on the mercy seat. The other became the scapegoat, upon which the high priest laid his hands and confessed all Israel's sins, then sent it into the wilderness, carrying away their sins. Both goats point to Christ, who is both the sacrifice and the one who bears our sins away.

Blood on the Mercy Seat

On the Day of Atonement, the high priest sprinkled blood on and before the mercy seat, the cover of the ark of the covenant. This blood made atonement for the sins of the people. The mercy seat was where God's presence dwelt, and the blood was the means of atonement. This points forward to how Christ's blood provides atonement, allowing us to approach God's presence.

And according to the law almost all things are purified with blood, and without shedding of blood there is no remission. Hebrews 9:22

The blood on the mercy seat was essential for atonement. Without it, the high priest would die in God's presence. This shows us that blood is required for atonementâ€”a truth fulfilled perfectly in Christ's blood. His blood provides the atonement that allows us to approach God's presence without fear.

Conclusion: Atonement Complete, Access Granted

The Day of Atonement teaches us that atonement is necessary, that blood must be shed, and that access to God requires a mediator. All of this finds its perfect fulfillment in Christ, who entered heaven as our High Priest, offered Himself as the perfect sacrifice, and provides eternal atonement.

Through Christ's work, we have what the Day of Atonement could only point to: complete, final, and eternal atonement. We no longer need an annual Day of Atonement because Christ has made atonement once for all. We can now approach God's throne with confidence, not with fear, because Christ's blood has provided the atonement we need.
