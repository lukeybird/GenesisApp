Christ Our Sin Offering: Bearing the Guilt of Sin

The sin offering in Leviticus 4 was specifically designed to atone for unintentional sins. Unlike the burnt offering which was completely consumed, portions of the sin offering were burned outside the camp, symbolizing the removal of sin and guilt from the presence of God. This points directly to Christ, who bore our sin and guilt outside the camp.

For the bodies of those animals, whose blood is brought into the sanctuary by the high priest for sin, are burned outside the camp. Therefore Jesus also, that He might sanctify the people with His own blood, suffered outside the gate. Hebrews 13:11-12

Christ was crucified outside the gates of Jerusalem, just as the sin offering was burned outside the camp. He bore our sin and guilt, taking upon Himself the punishment we deserved. The sin offering that was consumed outside the camp points to Christ who was made sin for us, that we might become the righteousness of God in Him.

The Blood Applied: Cleansing from Sin

In the sin offering, the blood was applied to the horns of the altar and poured at its base (Leviticus 4:7). This blood application was essential for atonement, pointing forward to the blood of Christ that cleanses us from all sin.

The Power of Christ's Blood

The blood of the sin offering atoned for sin, but it was temporary and had to be repeated. Christ's blood, however, was shed once for all, providing eternal atonement for all who believe. His blood cleanses us from sin, removes our guilt, and reconciles us to God.

For if the blood of bulls and goats and the ashes of a heifer, sprinkling the unclean, sanctifies for the purifying of the flesh, how much more shall the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered Himself without spot to God, cleanse your conscience from dead works to serve the living God? Confession and Laying On of Hands

The sin offering required the sinner to lay hands on the head of the animal and confess the sin (Leviticus 5:5). This act symbolized the transfer of sin and guilt from the sinner to the sacrifice, pointing to how our sin was transferred to Christ.

All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned, every one, to his own way; and the Lord has laid on Him the iniquity of us all. Isaiah 53:6

When we confess our sin and trust in Christ, our sin is transferred to Him. He bore the penalty we deserved. The laying on of hands in the sin offering points to this transferâ€”our sin laid on Christ, His righteousness given to us.

Conclusion: Sin Removed, Guilt Gone

The sin offering teaches us that sin requires atonement, that blood must be shed, and that sin must be removed from God's presence. All of this finds its perfect fulfillment in Christ, who became our sin offering, bearing our guilt and removing our sin.

Through Christ's sacrifice, our sin is not merely coveredâ€”it is removed. As far as the east is from the west, so far has He removed our transgressions from us (Psalm 103:12). The sin offering that was burned outside the camp points to Christ who bore our sin outside the city, that we might be brought near to God.
