The story of the burning bush in Exodus 3:1-4:17 marks a pivotal moment in redemptive history. When God reveals His name as "I AM WHO I AM" (Exodus 3:14), He is revealing His eternal, self-existent nature. This name finds its ultimate fulfillment in Jesus Christ, who declared Himself to be the great "I AM."

When Jesus declared "I AM," He was claiming to be the same eternal God who appeared to Moses in the burning bush. The Burning Bush: Divine Presence Without Consumption

The burning bush that was not consumed (Exodus 3:2) is a powerful picture of Christ's incarnation. The bush burned with fire yet was not consumed, just as Christ is fully God (the fire of divine presence) yet fully man (the bush that was not destroyed). The Mystery of the Incarnation

The burning bush reveals a truth about God's nature that finds its ultimate expression in Christ: God can be present in the material world without being consumed by it or consuming it. In Christ, "all the fullness of the Deity lives in bodily form" (Colossians 2:9), yet He remained fully human.

Moses the Deliverer: A Type of Christ the Deliverer

God called Moses to deliver Israel from slavery in Egypt (Exodus 3:10). Moses was sent by God to bring God's people out of bondage, just as Christ was sent by the Father to deliver us from the bondage of sin.

God's Promise: "I Will Be With You"

Conclusion: The I AM Who Delivers

The burning bush reveals the eternal God who came down to deliver His people. The "I AM" who appeared to Moses is the same "I AM" who came in the flesh as Jesus Christ. Just as God called Moses to deliver Israel from physical slavery, so Christ came to deliver us from spiritual slavery.

The burning bush that was not consumed points to Christ, who is fully God yet fully man. The holy ground where Moses stood points to the holiness we receive through Christ. And God's promise to be with Moses finds its ultimate fulfillment in Christ, who is "God with us" forever.
