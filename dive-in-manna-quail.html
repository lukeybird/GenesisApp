Seeing Christ as the Bread from Heaven

Christ: The True Bread from Heaven

When God provided manna from heaven for Israel in the wilderness (Exodus 16:1-36), He was providing daily bread to sustain His people. This manna points to Christ, who declared Himself to be "the bread of life" that came down from heaven to give eternal life to all who believe in Him.

Then Jesus said to them, "Most assuredly, I say to you, Moses did not give you the bread from heaven, but My Father gives you the true bread from heaven. For the bread of God is He who comes down from heaven and gives life to the world." John 6:32-33

Jesus explicitly connects Himself with the manna from heaven. Just as the manna came down from heaven to sustain Israel in the wilderness, so Christ came down from heaven to give eternal life to the world. The manna was a temporary provision that perished; Christ is the eternal provision that gives everlasting life. The manna satisfied physical hunger for a day; Christ satisfies spiritual hunger forever.

Daily Bread: Sustenance from Heaven

The manna appeared each morning, and the people were to gather only enough for that day (Exodus 16:4, 16-20). If they tried to save it for the next day, it would rot. This daily provision points to our need to daily feed on Christ through His Word, prayer, and communion. We cannot live on yesterday's manna; we need fresh bread from heaven each day.

The Daily Need

Just as Israel needed to gather manna fresh each morning, so we need to feed on Christ daily. The manna that was saved for the next day rotted, teaching us that we cannot live on past experiences with Christ. We need daily fellowship with Him, daily feeding on His Word, daily communion with Him. Christ is our daily bread, and we must come to Him each day for fresh sustenance.

Matthew 6:11

The daily provision of manna teaches us about our daily need for Christ. We cannot store up enough spiritual food to last us for days or weeks. We need to come to Christ each day, to feed on Him through His Word, to commune with Him in prayer, to remember Him in the Lord's Supper. Just as the manna sustained Israel day by day, so Christ sustains us day by day.

Feeding on Christ: The Bread of Life

Jesus declared that we must "eat His flesh and drink His blood" to have eternal life (John 6:53-54). This shocking statement points to the necessity of feeding on Christ by faith. Just as Israel had to eat the manna to live, so we must feed on Christ to have eternal life.

Then Jesus said to them, "Most assuredly, I say to you, unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink His blood, you have no life in you. Whoever eats My flesh and drinks My blood has eternal life, and I will raise him up at the last day." John 6:53-54

To "eat the flesh" and "drink the blood" of Christ means to receive Him by faith, to feed on Him spiritually, to make Him our sustenance. Just as the manna was the food that sustained Israel in the wilderness, so Christ is the food that sustains us in our spiritual journey. We must feed on Him daily through His Word, through prayer, through communion, through fellowship with Him.

The Bread That Perishes vs. The Bread That Endures

The manna that God provided in the wilderness was miraculous, but it was temporary. It perished if kept overnight, and those who ate it eventually died. This points to the contrast between temporary, physical provision and eternal, spiritual provision in Christ.

Do not labor for the food which perishes, but for the food which endures to everlasting life, which the Son of Man will give you, because God the Father has set His seal on Him. John 6:27

Jesus contrasts the manna that perished with Himself, the bread that endures to everlasting life. The manna satisfied physical hunger temporarily; Christ satisfies spiritual hunger eternally. The manna could not prevent death; Christ gives eternal life. The manna was a type pointing to the true bread; Christ is the fulfillment of that type.
