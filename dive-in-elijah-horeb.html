God Speaks in a Whisper

Elijah's Flight

In 1 Kings 19:1-18, after Elijah's victory on Mount Carmel, Queen Jezebel threatens to kill him. Elijah flees in fear, but God meets him at Mount Horeb in a gentle whisper.

"Elijah was afraid and ran for his life. When he came to Beersheba in Judah, he left his servant there, while he himself went a day's journey into the wilderness. He came to a broom bush, sat down under it and prayed that he might die. 'I have had enough, Lord,' he said. 'Take my life; I am no better than my ancestors.'" 1 Kings 19:3-4

After his great victory on Mount Carmel, Elijah should be celebrating. Instead, when Jezebel threatens to kill him, he flees in fear. He goes to Beersheba and then into the wilderness, where he asks God to take his life. Despite his recent victory, Elijah feels alone and defeated. An angel provides food and water for Elijah, and he travels for forty days to Mount Horeb, the mountain of God.

God's Gentle Whisper

At Mount Horeb, God appears to Elijah, but not in the spectacular ways one might expect. Instead, God speaks in a gentle whisper, demonstrating that His presence is not always in the spectacular.

"The Lord said, 'Go out and stand on the mountain in the presence of the Lord, for the Lord is about to pass by.' Then a great and powerful wind tore the mountains apart and shattered the rocks before the Lord, but the Lord was not in the wind. After the wind there was an earthquake, but the Lord was not in the earthquake. After the earthquake came a fire, but the Lord was not in the fire. And after the fire came a gentle whisper." 1 Kings 19:11-12

God could have appeared in the wind, earthquake, or fire, but He chose to speak in a gentle whisper. This demonstrates that God's presence is not always in the spectacular. Sometimes, God works in quiet and gentle ways. God reassures Elijah that he is not aloneâ€”there are still 7,000 in Israel who have not bowed to Baal. God also commissions Elijah to anoint new kings and a new prophet, Elisha.
