Seeing Christ in the First Promise of Redemption

The First Gospel: The Protoevangelium

The Fall in Genesis 3 is not merely the story of how sin entered the worldâ€”it is the story that immediately points forward to the solution. Even in the moment of judgment, God speaks the first promise of redemption, a promise that finds its fulfillment in Jesus Christ.

And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your seed and her Seed; He shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise His heel. Genesis 3:15

This verse, known as the Protoevangelium (first gospel), contains the first promise of a coming Redeemer. The "Seed of the woman" who will crush the serpent's head points directly to Jesus Christ, who would defeat Satan through His death and resurrection.

Additional References
Romans 16:20
"And the God of peace will crush Satan under your feet shortly," showing the fulfillment of Genesis 3:15 through Christ and His people.
Galatians 4:4
"But when the fullness of the time had come, God sent forth His Son, born of a woman," showing Christ as the promised Seed of the woman.
Luke 1:35
The angel tells Mary "that Holy One who is to be born will be called the Son of God," showing Christ as the unique Seed born of a woman without a human father.

The Serpent and the Savior

The serpent in the Garden represents Satan, the deceiver. The New Testament explicitly identifies the serpent with the devil: "So the great dragon was cast out, that serpent of old, called the Devil and Satan, who deceives the whole world" (Revelation 12:9). Jesus came to destroy the works of the devil and reverse the curse of the Fall.

He who sins is of the devil, for the devil has sinned from the beginning. For this purpose the Son of God was manifested, that He might destroy the works of the devil. 1 John 3:8
The Defeat of the Serpent

Jesus' victory over Satan is described throughout the New Testament. When Jesus was tempted in the wilderness (Matthew 4:1-11), He overcame the same tempter who deceived Eve. Through His perfect obedience, Jesus reversed Adam's disobedience. On the cross, Jesus crushed the serpent's head, defeating sin, death, and the power of the devil.

Colossians 2:15
Additional References
Luke 10:18
Jesus said "I saw Satan fall like lightning from heaven," showing His authority over the serpent who deceived Eve.
John 12:31
"Now is the judgment of this world; now the ruler of this world will be cast out," showing Christ's victory over Satan on the cross.
Hebrews 2:14
Christ came "that through death He might destroy him who had the power of death, that is, the devil," fulfilling the promise to crush the serpent's head.

The First Death: Covering of Skins

After the Fall, God made garments of skin for Adam and Eve (Genesis 3:21). This required the death of an animalâ€”the first death in Scripture. This act foreshadows the ultimate covering that would come through the death of Christ, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world.

The next day John saw Jesus coming toward him, and said, "Behold! The Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world!" John 1:29

The animal skins that covered Adam and Eve's shame were a temporary solution pointing to the permanent solution: the perfect sacrifice of Christ, whose blood covers our sin and removes our shame before God.

Additional References
Isaiah 61:10
"He has covered me with the robe of righteousness," showing that Christ's righteousness covers us, just as the skins covered Adam and Eve.
Revelation 7:14
The redeemed have "washed their robes and made them white in the blood of the Lamb," showing the ultimate covering through Christ's sacrifice.
Zechariah 3:3-4
The vision of Joshua's filthy garments being replaced with rich robes foreshadows how Christ removes our shame and clothes us in righteousness.

The Curse and the Cross

God pronounced curses as consequences of the Fall: on the serpent, on the woman, and on the man (Genesis 3:14-19). These curses affected all creation. But the New Testament reveals that Christ took the curse upon Himself to redeem us from it.

Christ has redeemed us from the curse of the law, having become a curse for us (for it is written, "Cursed is everyone who hangs on a tree"). Galatians 3:13
Bearing the Curse

On the cross, Jesus bore the full weight of the curse. He experienced the thorns (part of the curse on the ground), the pain (part of the curse on humanity), and ultimately death itself. By becoming a curse for us, He freed us from the curse of the Fall and opened the way back to the tree of life.

Revelation 22:2
Additional References
Deuteronomy 21:23
"He who is hanged is accursed of God," the verse Paul quotes in Galatians 3:13, showing that Christ literally became a curse for us.
Isaiah 53:4-5
"He has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows... He was wounded for our transgressions," showing Christ bearing the full weight of the curse.
2 Corinthians 5:21
"For He made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us," showing that Christ took upon Himself the curse of sin that we deserved.

Disobedience and Obedience

Adam's one act of disobedience brought sin and death into the world. The New Testament explicitly contrasts this with Christ's one act of obedience that brings righteousness and life.

For as by one man's disobedience many were made sinners, so also by one Man's obedience many will be made righteous. Romans 5:19

Where Adam chose his own will over God's will, Jesus chose God's will even when it meant the cross. In the Garden of Gethsemane, Jesus faced the ultimate test and said, "Not My will, but Yours, be done" (Luke 22:42). His perfect obedience reversed the effects of Adam's disobedience.

Additional References
Philippians 2:8
Christ "became obedient to the point of death, even the death of the cross," showing perfect obedience in contrast to Adam's disobedience.
Hebrews 5:8-9
"Though He was a Son, yet He learned obedience by the things which He suffered. And having been perfected, He became the author of eternal salvation," showing that obedience was learned and perfected.
John 6:38
"For I have come down from heaven, not to do My own will, but the will of Him who sent Me," showing Christ's consistent commitment to God's will.

Separation and Reconciliation

After the Fall, Adam and Eve were driven out of the Garden and separated from the presence of God (Genesis 3:23-24). This separation represents the spiritual death that came through sin. But through Christ, we are reconciled back to God.

For if when we were enemies we were reconciled to God through the death of His Son, much more, having been reconciled, we shall be saved by His life. Romans 5:10
Access Restored

Through His death and resurrection, Jesus tore down the barrier that separated us from God. The veil of the temple was torn in two (Matthew 27:51), symbolizing that access to God's presence is now open. We who were once far off have been brought near by the blood of Christ (Ephesians 2:13).

Additional References
2 Corinthians 5:18-19
"All things are of God, who has reconciled us to Himself through Jesus Christ... God was in Christ reconciling the world to Himself," showing the restoration of relationship.
Colossians 1:20-22
"Having made peace through the blood of His cross... to reconcile all things to Himself... you, who once were alienated... He has reconciled."
Hebrews 10:19-20
"We have confidence to enter the Holiest by the blood of Jesus, by a new and living way," showing that access to God's presence is restored.

Death and Life

The Fall brought death into the world: "For dust you are, and to dust you shall return" (Genesis 3:19). But Christ came to destroy death and bring life.

The last enemy that will be destroyed is death. 1 Corinthians 15:26
Jesus said to her, "I am the resurrection and the life. He who believes in Me, though he may die, he shall live." John 11:25

Through His own death and resurrection, Jesus conquered death. He is the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep (1 Corinthians 15:20), guaranteeing that all who are in Him will also be raised to eternal life.

Additional References
1 Corinthians 15:54-55
"Death is swallowed up in victory. O Death, where is your sting? O Hades, where is your victory?" showing the complete defeat of death through Christ.
2 Timothy 1:10
Christ "has abolished death and brought life and immortality to light through the gospel," reversing the curse of death from the Fall.
Revelation 1:18
"I am He who lives, and was dead, and behold, I am alive forevermore. Amen. And I have the keys of Hades and of Death," showing Christ's authority over death.

The Tree of Life: Access Restored

After the Fall, God placed cherubim and a flaming sword to guard the way to the tree of life (Genesis 3:24). Humanity was cut off from eternal life. But the book of Revelation reveals that through Christ, access to the tree of life is restored.

Blessed are those who do His commandments, that they may have the right to the tree of life, and may enter through the gates into the city. Revelation 22:14

In the new creation, the tree of life will be available to all who are in Christ. The way that was blocked in Eden is now open through the cross. Jesus Himself is the way, the truth, and the life (John 14:6), and through Him we have access to eternal life.

Additional References
Revelation 22:2
"In the middle of its street, and on either side of the river, was the tree of life," showing the tree of life restored in the new creation.
Revelation 22:19
"And if anyone takes away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God shall take away his part from the Book of Life, from the holy city, and from the things which are written in this book," showing the connection between the tree of life and eternal life.
John 10:9
"I am the door. If anyone enters by Me, he will be saved," showing that Christ is the way to eternal life, just as the tree of life was the way in Eden.

Conclusion: From Fall to Redemption

The Fall is not the end of the storyâ€”it is the beginning of God's plan of redemption. Every element of the Fall points forward to Christ: the serpent's defeat, the covering for sin, the bearing of the curse, the reversal of disobedience, the restoration of relationship, the defeat of death, and the restoration of access to life.

When we read Genesis 3, we are reading about the problem that Christ came to solve. The Fall shows us our need for a Savior, and the first promise of redemption (Genesis 3:15) points us to the One who would fulfill it. The story of the Fall is, from the very first promise, the story of Christ.

Additional References
1 Timothy 2:5-6
"For there is one God and one Mediator between God and men, the Man Christ Jesus, who gave Himself a ransom for all," showing Christ as the solution to the problem introduced in the Fall.
Hebrews 9:15
"And for this reason He is the Mediator of the new covenant... that those who are called may receive the promise of the eternal inheritance," showing the fulfillment of the first promise of redemption.
1 John 3:8
"For this purpose the Son of God was manifested, that He might destroy the works of the devil," showing that Christ came specifically to reverse what happened in the Fall.
