The Warning of Final Judgment

The destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah serves as a warning about the final judgment that is coming. Jesus Himself used this event as a warning about His return.

But on the day that Lot went out of Sodom it rained fire and brimstone from heaven and destroyed them all. Even so will it be in the day when the Son of Man is revealed. Lucas 17:29-30

Just as Sodom was destroyed for its wickedness, so the world will face final judgment. But just as Lot was rescued, so those who are in Christ will be saved from the coming judgment.

Lot's Rescue: Salvation from Judgment

Lot was rescued from Sodom's destruction, not because of his own righteousness, but because God remembered Abraham. This points to how we are saved from judgmentâ€”not by our own righteousness, but through Christ, who is our righteousness.

Saved by Another's Righteousness

Lot was saved because of Abraham's intercession. We are saved because of Christ's intercession and His perfect righteousness credited to us. We are rescued from judgment not by our own merit, but by the merit of Another.

Lot's rescue shows that God can save the righteous from judgment. Through Christ, we are made righteous and rescued from the judgment to come.

Conclusion: The Intercessor and the Judge

The story of Sodom and Gomorrah shows both God's judgment on sin and His power to save the righteous. Abraham's intercession points to Christ, our Intercessor. Lot's rescue points to our rescue through Christ. And the warning of judgment points to the final judgment, from which only Christ can save us.

But there is a crucial difference: Abraham's intercession could not ultimately save Sodom, but Christ's intercession can and does save all who come to Him in faith.
