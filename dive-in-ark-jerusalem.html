The Center of Worship Established

The First Attempt: Learning God's Ways

In 1 Chronicles 13:1-14, David decides to bring the ark of the covenant to Jerusalem. However, on the first attempt, they transport it on a cart instead of carrying it on poles as God commanded. When the oxen stumble, Uzzah reaches out to steady the ark and is struck down by God. This teaches David and all Israel the importance of following God's instructions exactly.

They moved the ark of God from Abinadab's house on a new cart, with Uzzah and Ahio guiding it. David and all the Israelites were celebrating with all their might before God, with songs and with harps, lyres, timbrels, cymbals and trumpets. When they came to the threshing floor of Kidon, Uzzah reached out his hand to steady the ark, because the oxen stumbled. The Lord's anger burned against Uzzah, and he struck him down because he had put his hand on the ark. So he died there before God. 1 Chronicles 13:7-10

Uzzah's death serves as a sobering reminder that God's holiness must be respected. The ark represented God's presence, and it was to be handled only according to God's specific instructions. This event points forward to how we must approach God through Christ, the only way to God's presence, and how we must worship in spirit and truth according to God's word.

The Second Attempt: Following God's Instructions

In 1 Chronicles 15:1-29, David learns from his mistake and brings the ark to Jerusalem the correct way. This time, the Levites carry the ark on poles as God commanded. David organizes worship, appoints musicians, and the ark is brought to Jerusalem with great celebration and joy.

The Ark in Jerusalem: God's Presence Among His People

With the ark now in Jerusalem, David establishes it as the center of worship. He appoints Levites to minister before the ark and organizes the worship services. The ark's presence in Jerusalem represents God dwelling among His people, pointing forward to how Christ dwells among us and how the church is the temple of the Holy Spirit.

So they brought the ark of God and set it inside the tent that David had pitched for it, and they presented burnt offerings and fellowship offerings before God. After David had finished sacrificing the burnt offerings and fellowship offerings, he blessed the people in the name of the Lord. 1 Chronicles 16:1-2

The ark's establishment in Jerusalem marks a new era in Israel's worship. God's presence is now centered in the city that will become the site of the temple. This points forward to how Christ is the true presence of God among us and how the church, as the body of Christ, is the temple where God's Spirit dwells.

Conclusion: Christ Brings Us Into God's Presence

David's bringing of the ark to Jerusalem demonstrates both the seriousness of approaching God in the right way and the joy of being in God's presence. The ark represented God's presence among His people, and it had to be handled according to God's specific instructions.

This event points forward to how Christ brings us into God's presence. Just as the ark had to be carried in the specific way God commanded, we must come to God through Christ, the only way to the Father. Through Christ, we can approach God's throne of grace with confidence, knowing that He has made a way for us to be in His presence forever.
