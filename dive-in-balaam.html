God Turns Curses into Blessings

The Attempted Curse: God's Protection

Balak, king of Moab, hired Balaam to curse Israel because he feared their power (Numbers 22:1-6). But God would not allow Balaam to curse what He had blessed. Despite Balak's repeated attempts and offers of reward, Balaam could only bless Israel. This shows God's sovereign protection of His people, pointing forward to how nothing can separate us from God's love in Christ.

How can I curse those whom God has not cursed? How can I denounce those whom the Lord has not denounced? Numbers 23:8

Balaam discovered that he could not curse what God had blessed. No amount of money, no number of attempts, could change God's blessing on Israel. This points forward to how nothing can separate us from God's love in Christ. We are blessed in Him, and no curse can overcome God's blessing.

Galatians 3:13-14
"Christ has redeemed us from the curse of the law... that the blessing of Abraham might come upon the Gentiles," showing that in Christ we receive God's blessing, just as Israel was blessed.

The Star and Scepter: Messianic Prophecy

In his fourth oracle, Balaam prophesied about a coming ruler: "A Star shall come out of Jacob; a Scepter shall rise out of Israel" (Numbers 24:17). This is one of the clearest messianic prophecies in the Old Testament, pointing forward to Christ, who would come from the line of Jacob and rule over all nations.

Christ the Star and Scepter

Balaam's prophecy about a Star and Scepter coming from Jacob points directly to Christ. He is the Star who brings light to the world, and He is the Scepter who rules over all. This prophecy, spoken by a pagan prophet, shows that God was revealing His plan of salvation even through unlikely means.

Revelation 22:16
I, Jesus, have sent My angel to testify to you these things in the churches. I am the Root and the Offspring of David, the Bright and Morning Star. Revelation 22:16

Balaam's prophecy about the Star and Scepter finds its fulfillment in Christ. He is the Bright and Morning Star, bringing light to a dark world. He is the Scepter, ruling over all nations. This prophecy, spoken by a pagan prophet hired to curse Israel, became one of the most beautiful prophecies of the coming Messiah.

Additional References
Matthew 2:2
The wise men saw "His star in the East," showing that Christ is the Star prophesied by Balaam.
Hebrews 1:8
"Your throne, O God, is forever and ever; a scepter of righteousness is the scepter of Your kingdom," showing that Christ is the Scepter who rules.
Psalm 110:2
"The Lord shall send the rod of Your strength out of Zion. Rule in the midst of Your enemies!" showing that Christ will rule as the Scepter.

The Speaking Donkey: God's Sovereign Control

On his way to meet Balak, Balaam's donkey saw an angel of the Lord blocking the path and turned aside three times. When Balaam beat the donkey, God opened its mouth to speak (Numbers 22:21-30). God's Sovereign Protection

God used a donkey to protect Balaam from the angel's sword and to remind him that he could only speak what God allowed. This shows that God is in complete control, even over those who oppose Him. He protects His people and ensures that His purposes are accomplished, no matter what obstacles arise.

Proverbs 21:1
The king's heart is in the hand of the Lord, like the rivers of water; He turns it wherever He wishes. Proverbs 21:1

Just as God controlled Balaam's donkey and prevented him from cursing Israel, God controls all things for the good of His people. Nothing can thwart His purposes. The speaking donkey is a powerful reminder that God is sovereign over all creation and will protect and bless those whom He has chosen.

Blessings Instead of Curses: God's Unchanging Purpose

Despite Balak's repeated attempts to get Balaam to curse Israel, Balaam could only bless them. In four separate oracles, Balaam pronounced blessings over Israel, declaring their unique status, their future greatness, and prophesying about the coming Messiah (Numbers 23-24). This shows that God's purposes cannot be changedâ€”what He has blessed remains blessed.

God's Unchanging Blessing

Balaam's inability to curse Israel demonstrates that God's blessing is unchangeable. In Christ, we are blessed with every spiritual blessing, and nothing can separate us from God's love. Just as Balaam could only bless Israel, so God's blessing on us in Christ is secure and eternal.

Ephesians 1:3
Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places in Christ. Ephesians 1:3

Just as Balaam could not curse what God had blessed, nothing can curse what God has blessed in Christ. We are blessed with every spiritual blessing, and no enemy, no circumstance, no power can change that. God's blessing on His people is as unchangeable as His blessing on Israel was unchangeable.

Conclusion: God Turns Curses into Blessings

The story of Balaam and Balak teaches us profound truths about God's sovereignty and protection of His people. Despite Balak's attempts to curse Israel, God turned every intended curse into a blessing.
