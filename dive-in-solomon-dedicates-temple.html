God's Glory Fills the Temple

The Ark Brought Into the Temple

In 2 Chronicles 5:1-14, when the temple is completed, Solomon brings the ark of the covenant into the Most Holy Place. As the priests withdraw, the glory of the Lord fills the temple so powerfully that the priests cannot perform their service. This moment marks the fulfillment of God's promise to dwell among His people.

When the priests came out of the Holy Place (for all the priests who were present had consecrated themselves, without regard to their divisions), and all the Levitical singers, Asaph, Heman, Jeduthun, and their sons and kinsmen, clothed in fine linen, with cymbals, harps, and lyres, standing east of the altar, and with them one hundred and twenty priests blowing trumpets in unison when the trumpeters and the singers were to make themselves heard with one voice to praise and to glorify the Lord, and when they lifted up their voice accompanied by trumpets and cymbals and instruments of music, and when they praised the Lord saying, "He indeed is good for His lovingkindness is everlasting," then the house, the house of the Lord, was filled with a cloud, so that the priests could not stand to minister because of the cloud, for the glory of the Lord filled the house of God. 2 Chronicles 5:11-14

The glory of the Lord filling the temple demonstrates God's presence among His people. This visible manifestation of God's glory points forward to how God's glory is fully revealed in Christ, who is the true temple and the perfect manifestation of God's presence.

Solomon's Dedicatory Prayer

In 2 Chronicles 6:1-42, Solomon prays a dedicatory prayer, asking God to hear the prayers of His people when they pray toward the temple. He acknowledges that God cannot be contained in a building, yet asks God to hear from heaven when His people pray.

Prayer and God's Presence

Solomon's prayer recognizes that while God fills the temple with His glory, He cannot be contained by it. This points forward to how through Christ, we can approach God in prayer from anywhere, knowing that He hears us through His Son.

Hebrews 4:16
But will God really dwell on earth with humans? The heavens, even the highest heavens, cannot contain you. How much less this temple I have built! Yet, Lord my God, give attention to your servant's prayer and his plea for mercy. Hear the cry and the prayer that your servant is praying in your presence. May your eyes be open toward this temple day and night, this place of which you said you would put your Name there. May you hear the prayer your servant prays toward this place. 2 Chronicles 6:18-20

Solomon's prayer demonstrates both humility and faith. He recognizes God's transcendence while asking for His immanence. This points forward to how through Christ, we have access to God's presence and can pray with confidence, knowing that He hears us.

God's Response and Promise

In 2 Chronicles 7:1-22, God appears to Solomon and promises to hear the prayers of His people. He also warns that if the people turn away, He will reject the temple. This demonstrates both God's faithfulness and His justice.

The Lord said to him: "I have heard your prayer and your plea, which you have made before me; I have consecrated this temple, which you have built, by putting my Name there forever. My eyes and my heart will always be there. As for you, if you walk before me faithfully as David your father did, and do all I command, and observe my decrees and laws, I will establish your royal throne, as I covenanted with David your father when I said, 'You shall never fail to have a successor to rule over Israel.'" 2 Chronicles 7:12-18

God's response to Solomon's prayer shows His commitment to His people and His temple. However, it also contains a warning about the consequences of unfaithfulness. This points forward to how Christ is the true temple, and through faith in Him, we have access to God's presence forever.

Conclusion: Christ, the True Temple

The dedication of Solomon's temple points forward to Christ, who is the true temple. Just as God's glory filled the physical temple, God's glory is fully revealed in Christ. Just as the temple was the place where God's people could approach Him, Christ is the way through which we approach God.

Through Christ, we have access to God's presence that is not limited to a physical location. We are the temple of the Holy Spirit, and through Christ, we can approach God's throne of grace with confidence. The dedication of Solomon's temple finds its ultimate fulfillment in Christ, who makes true worship possible.
