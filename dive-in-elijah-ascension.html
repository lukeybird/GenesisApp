The Prophet Taken Up, The Successor Empowered

Elijah's Departure

In 2 Kings 2:1-18, Elijah is taken up to heaven in a whirlwind, and Elisha receives a double portion of his spirit. This demonstrates God's power and points forward to Christ's ascension.

"As they were walking along and talking together, suddenly a chariot of fire and horses of fire appeared and separated the two of them, and Elijah went up to heaven in a whirlwind. Elisha saw this and cried out, 'My father! My father! The chariots and horsemen of Israel!' And Elisha saw him no more." 2 Kings 2:11-12

As Elijah and Elisha travel together, Elijah knows that the Lord is about to take him away. Elisha refuses to leave Elijah's side, even when Elijah tells him to stay behind. When they reach the Jordan River, Elijah strikes the water with his cloak, and the river parts, allowing them to cross on dry ground. After they cross, a chariot of fire and horses of fire appear, and Elijah is taken up to heaven in a whirlwind. Elisha sees this and tears his clothes in grief, crying out, "My father! My father! The chariots and horsemen of Israel!"

Elisha's Inheritance

After Elijah is taken up, Elisha picks up Elijah's cloak and strikes the Jordan River, and the water parts again. The prophets recognize that the spirit of Elijah now rests on Elisha.

"He took up the cloak that had fallen from Elijah and went back and stood on the bank of the Jordan. Then he took the cloak that had fallen from him and struck the water with it. 'Where now is the Lord, the God of Elijah?' he asked. When he struck the water, it divided to the right and to the left, and he crossed over." 2 Kings 2:13-14

Elisha picks up Elijah's cloak, which had fallen from him, and strikes the Jordan River. The water parts, and Elisha crosses over on dry ground. The prophets who were watching from a distance recognize that the spirit of Elijah now rests on Elisha. They come to meet him and bow down to the ground before him. Elisha receives a double portion of Elijah's spirit, as he had requested. This demonstrates God's power to empower His servants and to continue His work through their successors.

Christ's Ascension

Elijah's ascension points forward to how Christ ascended to heaven after His resurrection. Just as Elijah was taken up, Christ was taken up to the Father. And just as Elisha received Elijah's spirit, we receive the Holy Spirit from Christ.

This same Jesus, who has been taken from you into heaven, will come back in the same way you have seen him go into heaven.'" Acts 1:9-11

Conclusion: The Succession

Elijah's ascension and Elisha's succession demonstrate God's power and faithfulness. This event points forward to how Christ ascended to heaven and how He empowers His followers through the Holy Spirit.

Elijah's ascension is a powerful demonstration of God's power. Just as God took Elijah up to heaven, God took Christ up to heaven after His resurrection. However, while Elijah was taken up in a whirlwind, Christ ascended in His own power, demonstrating His divinity.

Just as Elisha received Elijah's spirit and continued his work, we receive the Holy Spirit from Christ and continue His work.
