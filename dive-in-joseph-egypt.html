Seeing Christ in Exaltation and Provision

From Prison to Palace: The Pattern of Exaltation

Joseph's rise from prison to second-in-command of Egypt in Genesis 39-41 points to Christ's exaltation from death to the right hand of God. Joseph was unjustly imprisoned but then exalted. Christ was unjustly crucified but then raised and exalted.

Therefore God also has highly exalted Him and given Him the name which is above every name, that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow. Philippians 2:9-10

The Bread of Life: Provision for All

Joseph stored grain during the years of plenty to provide for the years of famine. He became the provider of bread for all of Egypt and the surrounding nations. This points to Christ, who is the Bread of Life, providing spiritual nourishment for all who come to Him.

And Jesus said to them, "I am the bread of life. He who comes to Me shall never hunger, and he who believes in Me shall never thirst." John 6:35

Joseph provided physical bread that saved people from physical death. Christ provides spiritual bread that saves us from spiritual death. He is the true Bread from heaven, the only food that satisfies the soul.

Wisdom and Discernment

Joseph's wisdom in interpreting dreams and managing Egypt's resources points to Christ, who is the wisdom of God. Pharaoh said of Joseph, "Can we find such a one as this, a man in whom is the Spirit of God?" (Genesis 41:38).

Conclusion: The Exalted Provider

Joseph's story shows how God exalts the humble and uses suffering for good. Joseph was exalted from prison to palace, and he provided for all who came to him. Christ was exalted from death to the throne, and He provides eternal life for all who come to Him.
