Seeing Christ in Judgment and Salvation

The Ark: A Picture of Salvation

The story of Noah and the Flood in Genesis 6-9 is one of the most powerful types and shadows of Christ in all of Scripture. The ark that saved Noah and his family from the floodwaters of judgment is a clear picture of how Christ saves us from the judgment we deserve.

For Christ also suffered once for sins, the just for the unjust, that He might bring us to God, being put to death in the flesh but made alive by the Spirit, by whom also He went and preached to the spirits in prison, who formerly were disobedient, when once the Divine longsuffering waited in the days of Noah, while the ark was being prepared, in which a few, that is, eight souls, were saved through water. There is also an antitype which now saves usâ€”baptism (not the removal of the filth of the flesh, but the answer of a good conscience toward God), through the resurrection of Jesus Christ. 1 Peter 3:18-21

The apostle Peter explicitly connects the ark to our salvation in Christ. Just as only those inside the ark were saved from the flood, so only those who are "in Christ" are saved from the judgment to come. The ark was the only means of salvation from the floodâ€”Christ is the only means of salvation from sin and judgment.

Noah's Righteousness: A Type of Christ's Righteousness

Genesis 6:9 tells us that "Noah was a just man, perfect in his generations. Noah walked with God." This righteousness was not Noah's ownâ€”it was a righteousness that came through faith, pointing forward to the perfect righteousness of Christ that is credited to us through faith.

Righteousness Through Faith

The writer of Hebrews includes Noah in the great "Hall of Faith" chapter, showing that Noah's righteousness came through faith: "By faith Noah, being divinely warned of things not yet seen, moved with godly fear, prepared an ark for the saving of his household, by which he condemned the world and became heir of the righteousness which is according to faith" (Hebrews 11:7).

Hebrews 11:7

Noah's righteousness, though real, was imperfect. But it points to the perfect righteousness of Christ, who alone was truly "just" and "perfect" in every way. Through faith in Christ, we receive His perfect righteousness, just as Noah received God's favor through faith.

The Door of the Ark: Christ the Only Way

When the flood came, Genesis 7:16 tells us that "the Lord shut him in." There was only one door to the ark, and once God closed it, there was no other way to be saved. This powerfully points to Christ, who declared Himself to be "the door" (John 10:9).

I am the door. If anyone enters by Me, he will be saved, and will go in and out and find pasture. John 10:9

Just as there was only one door to the ark, there is only one way to salvationâ€”through Jesus Christ. Those outside the ark perished; those outside of Christ face eternal judgment. The door of the ark was God's provision for salvation; Christ is God's provision for our salvation.

The Flood: Judgment and the Final Judgment

The Flood was God's judgment on a world that had become completely corrupt. Genesis 6:5 tells us that "every intent of the thoughts of [man's] heart was only evil continually." This judgment points forward to the final judgment that is coming, and to the only refuge from that judgmentâ€”Jesus Christ.

But as the days of Noah were, so also will the coming of the Son of Man be. For as in the days before the flood, they were eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage, until the day that Noah entered the ark, and did not know until the flood came and took them all away, so also will the coming of the Son of Man be. Matthew 24:37-39

Jesus Himself used the Flood as a warning about the coming judgment. Just as the world was unprepared for the flood, so many will be unprepared for Christ's return. But just as the ark provided refuge from the flood, so Christ provides refuge from the coming judgment.

The Rainbow: The Covenant of Grace

After the Flood, God made a covenant with Noah and all creation, promising never again to destroy the earth with a flood. The rainbow was given as a sign of this covenant (Genesis 9:12-17). This covenant points forward to the new covenant in Christ, sealed not with a rainbow but with His blood.

This is My blood of the new covenant, which is shed for many for the remission of sins. Matthew 26:28

The rainbow covenant was a promise of God's mercy and faithfulness. The new covenant in Christ is the ultimate expression of God's mercy and faithfulness, providing not just protection from physical destruction, but salvation from sin and eternal death.

Additional References

The promise of a new covenant, fulfilled in Christ, which provides forgiveness of sins and a new heart.
Revelation 4:3
In heaven, there is "a rainbow around the throne," reminding us of God's covenant faithfulness, ultimately fulfilled in Christ.

Conclusion: Finding Refuge in Christ

The story of the Flood is a powerful reminder that judgment is coming, but God has provided a way of escape. The ark that saved Noah points to Christ, the true ark of salvation. Just as Noah had to enter the ark to be saved, so we must enter into Christ through faith to be saved from the judgment to come.

Noah's righteousness through faith points to the righteousness we receive through faith in Christ. The one door of the ark points to Christ, the only door to salvation. The flood of judgment points to the final judgment, from which only Christ can save us. And the rainbow covenant points to the new covenant in Christ, sealed with His blood.
