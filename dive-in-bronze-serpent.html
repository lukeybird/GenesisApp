The Perfect Picture: Christ Lifted Up

The bronze serpent in Numbers 21:4-9 is one of the clearest pictures of Christ in the Old Testament. When the people were bitten by venomous snakes, God instructed Moses to make a bronze serpent and lift it up on a pole. Anyone who looked at it would live. This points directly to how Christ was lifted up on the cross, and all who look to Him in faith receive eternal life.

And as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have eternal life. John 3:14-15

Jesus Himself explicitly connected the bronze serpent to His crucifixion. Just as the bronze serpent was lifted up on a pole, Christ was lifted up on the cross. Just as looking at the bronze serpent brought healing from the snake bites, looking to Christ in faith brings healing from sin and eternal life. The bronze serpent is a perfect picture of the gospelâ€”simple, accessible, and effective for all who look.
