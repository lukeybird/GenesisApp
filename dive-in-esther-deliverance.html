Evil Defeated, God's People Delivered

Haman's Downfall

In Esther 6:1-7:10, on the night before Esther's second banquet, the king cannot sleep and has the chronicles read to him. He learns that Mordecai had once saved his life but was never rewarded. The next day, Haman comes to ask permission to hang Mordecai, but the king asks Haman what should be done for someone the king wants to honor. Thinking the king means him, Haman suggests an elaborate honor. The king then commands Haman to honor Mordecai in this way. At the second banquet, Esther reveals that she is a Jew and that Haman has plotted to destroy her people. The king is enraged and orders Haman to be hanged on the gallows he had prepared for Mordecai. Haman's downfall demonstrates that evil will be defeated and that God's people will be delivered.

So they hanged Haman on the gallows that he had prepared for Mordecai. Then the king's wrath subsided. Esther 7:10
Evil Defeated

Haman's downfall demonstrates that evil will be defeated and that God's people will be delivered. The very instrument of destruction that Haman prepared for Mordecai becomes the instrument of his own destruction. This points forward to how Christ defeated Satan through the cross, the very instrument Satan intended to use to destroy Christ.

The Jews' Victory

In Esther 8:1-9:32, after Haman's execution, Esther is given Haman's estate, and Mordecai is elevated to Haman's position. The king issues a new decree allowing the Jews to defend themselves, and they are victorious over their enemies. The Jews' deliverance demonstrates God's providential care and points forward to how Christ delivers His people from sin and death.

The Jews had light and gladness, joy and honor. And in every province and every city, wherever the king's command and decree came, the Jews had joy and gladness, a feast and a holiday. Then many of the people of the land became Jews, because fear of the Jews fell upon them. Esther 8:16-17
God's People Delivered

The Jews' deliverance from Haman's plot points forward to how Christ delivers His people from sin and death. Just as the Jews were saved from destruction, we are saved from eternal destruction through Christ. The victory of the Jews demonstrates God's faithfulness to His people.

Conclusion: Deliverance and Victory

The Jews' deliverance from Haman's plot demonstrates God's providential care and faithfulness to His people. Their victory points forward to how Christ delivers His people from sin and death and gives them victory over their enemies. Just as the Jews celebrated their deliverance with feasting and joy, we celebrate our deliverance in Christ with eternal joy.
