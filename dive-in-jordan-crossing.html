Entering the Promised Land

A New Beginning: Crossing into the Inheritance

In Joshua 3-4, Joshua leads Israel across the Jordan River on dry ground, just as Moses led them through the Red Sea. This miraculous crossing marks the beginning of the conquest of the Promised Land and demonstrates God's faithfulness to His promises. The crossing represents a new beginningâ€”leaving behind the wilderness and entering into the inheritance God had promised to Abraham centuries earlier.

And it came to pass, when the people set out from their tents to cross over the Jordan, with the priests bearing the ark of the covenant before the people, and as those who bore the ark came to the Jordan, and the feet of the priests who bore the ark dipped in the edge of the water (for the Jordan overflows all its banks during the whole time of harvest), that the waters which came down from upstream stood still, and rose in a heap very far away at Adam, the city that is beside Zaretan. So the waters that went down into the Sea of the Arabah, the Salt Sea, failed, and were cut off; and the people crossed over opposite Jericho. Joshua 3:14-16

The crossing of the Jordan demonstrates God's power over creation and His faithfulness to His promises. Just as God parted the Red Sea to deliver Israel from Egypt, He parts the Jordan to bring them into the Promised Land. This miracle shows that God is with Joshua just as He was with Moses, and that He will fulfill His promise to give Israel the land.

"All were baptized into Moses in the cloud and in the sea," showing that the Red Sea crossing was a type of baptism, just as the Jordan crossing points to our baptism into Christ.

The Ark Goes Before: God's Presence Leads the Way

In the crossing of the Jordan, the ark of the covenant goes before the people, carried by the priests. The ark represents God's presence, and its position at the front shows that God Himself is leading Israel into the Promised Land. The people are instructed to keep a distance of about two thousand cubits from the ark, showing reverence for God's holy presence.

God's Presence in the Lead

The ark going before Israel shows that God's presence leads His people. In the New Testament, Christ is described as going before us, leading us into our inheritance. Just as the ark led Israel into the Promised Land, Christ leads us into the kingdom of God.

Hebrews 6:20
Where the forerunner has entered for us, even Jesus, having become High Priest forever according to the order of Melchizedek. Hebrews 6:20

Just as the ark went before Israel into the Promised Land, Christ has gone before us into heaven. He is our forerunner, our High Priest who has entered the presence of God on our behalf. The ark's position at the front of the procession points to how Christ leads us into our eternal inheritance.

Memorial Stones: Remembering God's Faithfulness

After crossing the Jordan, Joshua commands twelve men, one from each tribe, to take twelve stones from the middle of the Jordan and set them up as a memorial at Gilgal. These stones serve as a permanent reminder of God's faithfulness in bringing Israel into the Promised Land. When future generations ask what the stones mean, they will be told of God's mighty act.

And these stones shall be for a memorial to the children of Israel forever... When your children ask their fathers in time to come, saying, "What are these stones?" then you shall let your children know, saying, "Israel crossed over this Jordan on dry land." Joshua 4:7, 21-22

The memorial stones point forward to how we remember God's faithfulness in Christ. The Lord's Supper serves as a memorial of Christ's death and resurrection, reminding us of how God brought us from death to life. Just as the stones reminded Israel of God's faithfulness, the Lord's Supper reminds us of God's faithfulness in Christ.

Baptism: Passing from Death to Life

The crossing of the Jordan points forward to baptism, where we pass through the waters of death into new life in Christ. Just as Israel left the wilderness behind and entered the Promised Land, we leave our old life behind and enter into the kingdom of God through faith in Christ.

Baptized into Christ

Baptism represents our identification with Christ in His death and resurrection. Just as Israel crossed through the Jordan into the Promised Land, we are baptized into Christ, passing from death to life. The waters of baptism, like the waters of the Jordan, represent the boundary between the old life and the new life.

Romans 6:4
Therefore we were buried with Him through baptism into death, that just as Christ was raised from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life. Romans 6:4

Just as Israel crossed the Jordan to enter the Promised Land, we are baptized into Christ to enter the kingdom of God. The crossing represents leaving the wilderness of sin and entering the inheritance God has prepared for us. Through faith in Christ, we cross from death to life, from the old creation to the new creation.

Additional References

Conclusion: Entering Our Inheritance in Christ

The crossing of the Jordan marks Israel's entry into the Promised Land, fulfilling God's promise to Abraham. This event points forward to how we enter our inheritance in Christ through faith. Just as Israel crossed the Jordan to receive the land, we cross from death to life through faith in Christ.

The ark going before Israel shows that God's presence leads His people. Christ has gone before us into heaven, and He leads us into our eternal inheritance. The memorial stones remind us to remember God's faithfulness, just as the Lord's Supper reminds us of Christ's work. The crossing of the Jordan ultimately points to how we enter the kingdom of God through faith in Christ, our greater Joshua.
