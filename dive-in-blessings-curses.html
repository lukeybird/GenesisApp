The choice before us

The Choice: Blessing or Curse

The blessings and curses in Leviticus 26 reveal the consequences of obedience and disobedience. For obedience, God promised blessing: rain in season, abundant crops, peace, victory, and His presence. For disobedience, He warned of curses: disease, defeat, famine, and exile. This stark choice reveals our fundamental problem: we cannot perfectly obey, so we deserve the curse. But Christ bore the curse for us, and in Him we receive every blessing.

Christ has redeemed us from the curse of the law, having become a curse for us (for it is written, "Cursed is everyone who hangs on a tree"), that the blessing of Abraham might come upon the Gentiles in Christ Jesus, that we might receive the promise of the Spirit through faith. Galatians 3:13-14

We were under the curse of the law because we could not perfectly obey. But Christ became a curse for us, hanging on the tree (the cross), so that we might receive the blessing. The blessings and curses of Leviticus reveal the choice we face, but Christ has made the choice for usâ€”He took the curse, and we receive the blessing.

God's Faithful Promise

Even in the midst of the curses, God promised that if Israel would confess their sins and humble themselves, He would remember His covenant. This reveals God's faithfulness and mercy, pointing forward to how God's promise is fulfilled in Christ. In Him, God's covenant is secure, and His promise to never forsake His people is guaranteed.

The Covenant Secured

The blessings and curses showed that Israel's relationship with God depended on their obedience. But in Christ, our relationship with God depends on His obedience. The covenant is secured by His work, not ours. God's promise to never forsake His people is fulfilled in Christ, who is the guarantee of the covenant.

Conclusion: From Curse to Blessing

The blessings and curses of Leviticus reveal the choice we face: blessing for obedience, curse for disobedience. But we cannot perfectly obey, so we deserve the curse. Christ, however, perfectly obeyed and yet took the curse upon Himself, so that we might receive the blessing.

In Christ, we are no longer under the curse but under the blessing. We receive every spiritual blessing, not because of our obedience, but because of His.
