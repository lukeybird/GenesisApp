Seeing Christ in Salvation Through the Waters

Baptism: Death to the Old Life

The crossing of the Red Sea in Exodus 14:1-31 is a powerful picture of baptism. Just as Israel passed through the waters of the Red Sea, leaving behind their old life of slavery in Egypt, so we pass through the waters of baptism, leaving behind our old life of sin and entering into new life in Christ.

Moreover, brethren, I do not want you to be unaware that all our fathers were under the cloud, all passed through the sea, all were baptized into Moses in the cloud and in the sea. 1 Corinthians 10:1-2

The apostle Paul explicitly connects the Red Sea crossing with baptism. Just as Israel was "baptized into Moses" by passing through the sea, so we are baptized into Christ by passing through the waters of baptism. The Red Sea crossing marked the end of Israel's old life in Egypt and the beginning of their new life as God's free people. Baptism marks the end of our old life in sin and the beginning of our new life in Christ.

From Death to Life: New Life in Christ

When Israel crossed the Red Sea, they left behind death (the Egyptian army) and entered into new life (freedom in the wilderness). The sea that was a barrier became a pathway, and the enemy that pursued them was destroyed. This points to how Christ brings us from death to life, from slavery to freedom.

Therefore we were buried with Him through baptism into death, that just as Christ was raised from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life. Romans 6:4
The Waters of Death and Life

The Red Sea was both a barrier and a pathway. For Israel, it was a pathway to freedom. For the Egyptians, it was a barrier to destruction. So baptism is both death to the old life and entrance into new life. We die to sin and self, and we rise to new life in Christ. The waters that represent death to our old life also represent the new life we have in Christ.

Romans 6:4-5

Just as Israel passed through the sea and emerged on the other side as a free people, so we pass through baptism and emerge as new creations in Christ. The old life of slavery is behind us; the new life of freedom is before us. Through Christ, we have passed from death to life, from the kingdom of darkness to the kingdom of light.

The Red Sea was the means of salvation for Israel. It was through the waters that they were saved from their enemy. This points to how the waters of baptism are the means by which we are saved and identified with Christ. The sea that was a barrier to Egypt became a pathway to freedom for Israel.
