The Timing: Christ Our Passover

The Passover lamb was to be killed "at twilight" on the fourteenth day of the month (Exodus 12:6). Remarkably, Christ was crucified at the very time when the Passover lambs were being slaughtered. He died as the true Passover Lamb, fulfilling the type perfectly.

The Meal: Fellowship with Christ

The Passover lamb was to be eaten with unleavened bread and bitter herbs (Exodus 12:8). The eating of the lamb signified participation in the deliverance. This points to our participation in Christ through faith and communion.

Then Jesus said to them, "Most assuredly, I say to you, unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink His blood, you have no life in you. Whoever eats My flesh and drinks My blood has eternal life, and I will raise him up at the last day." John 6:53-54

Just as Israel had to eat the Passover lamb to participate in the deliverance, so we must "feed on Christ" by faith to participate in His deliverance. The Lord's Supper, which Christ instituted at the Passover meal, reminds us that we must continually feed on Him. We partake of Christ through faith, receiving His life into ours.
